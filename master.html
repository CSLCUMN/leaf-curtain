Sample Home Page

This is one way that a home page might look in the University of Minnesota Drupal base theme.

Banners

You can choose from these banner options after the required University of Minnesota header:

  • a single banner for the site name
  • two banners, one for a parent unit and a one for a sub-unit, as shown on this page

Both can accommodate text or an image.

The single and parent unit banners can include optional icons from these Social Networking Graphics or Fontello. See the Sample Collegiate Site gold banner for an example. Please note that social media icon colors should not be changed unless this is allowed by the vendor. An administrator who wants change the placement of the social icons will need to do so manually.

Menus

You can include horizontal navigation below the banner(s) with optional hover dropdowns to subpages that function like the navigation on the Academic Health Center Resource Hub.

If you use dropdowns, the subpages should also be linked in a vertical navigation menu on the subpages, or in a sub-footer like the one on this page, because the dropdowns are not accessible to all users.

The vertical navigation menu can also be used on its own as the primary menu, and can accommodate up to two levels. The second level will show only when viewing one of the subpages in that section.

  • To see it used as a secondary menu, see the Sample Subpage. The menus initially will be closed on a mobile device, and display at full width at top and at bottom when opened.
  • To see it used as a primary menu, see the Sample Collegiate Site. The menu initially will be closed on a mobile device and display beneath the banner when opened.
  • You also may choose an alternate style for the vertical menu.

'Hero' Image or Carousel

You can choose to include an image with or without a caption on the home page, called a 'hero' image. Users who want to create rotating images or stories ('sliders' or 'carousels') will need to enable the Flexslider module and complete additional theming work. Please be aware that rotating images are not completely accessible to all users and University of Minnesota home page web analytics indicate that less than 1% of users view additional images presented this way.

You can choose to present the caption to the left of the image, as on this page, or below the image.
